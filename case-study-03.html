Product Design for an AI-Powered SaaS Speech Writing Tool

UX UI DESIGN

Role

UX UI Designer

Team

Product Owner, Engineers, UX Designer

Duration

4 months

We noticed that pastors could use a little help crafting their sermons. With substantial workloads, pastors invest extensive hours crafting engaging sermons.

We set out to create an AI-powered tool to act as a creative companion for pastors and potentially revolutionize their sermon crafting experience.

As part of a globally dispersed team, I led every facet of product design, spanning strategy and research to prototyping and visual design.

Problem

How can we reduce the time pastors spend on formulating and crafting sermon outlines?

It's reported that pastors struggle to consistently create quality and impactful sermons with with more than a half feeling overworked.

45%

of pastors spend 10 to 15 hours a week on sermon preparation.

© 2016, (research from 1989 to 2016, ongoing) Schaeffer Institute of leadership Development, lead researcher Rev. Richard J. Krejcir, Ph.D., www.churchleadership.org

SOLUTION

Software that kickstarts the creative process.

With a long history of serving the church community through technical products, my client identified the untapped potential of using AI to assist pastors in the area identified.

Our solution was to create a web application using GPT-4 that would transform the sermon theme creation process into a dynamic collaboration between a pastor's initial insights and the power of AI.

The primary objective was to enhance user satisfaction by simplifying and automating aspects of their process. It needed to be easy to learn with a focus on efficiency which would be achieved through an intuitive user interface.

UX Reseach

Competitor Analysis

I began by finding speech writing tools like Speech Formula and Speech Generator to learn common ways apps offer users to compose their speeches. I continued with reviewing tools that incorporate AI into their tools, such as Evernote, Jasper and Grammarly. This helped me evaluate common design patterns, including good and bad examples of user experience. It also allowed me begin formulating mental models which may be most suitable for our users.

Discovery interviews to gather insights and develop empathy.

I conducted remote discovery interviews with stakeholders to gather information about pastors they have worked with. This helped me to empathize with our users and also understand which tools and workflows they might use during sermon creation. This firsthand insight into the unique approaches and needs of pastors informed the design and functionality of PulpitPro.

As part of the user research I chose to create a proto persona based on stakeholder knowledge. It was the best solution for the budget and timeframe and it helped the team with brainstorming. It also ensured that everyone was on the same page regarding the user needs and aided with user stories.

Technical AI Review

I performed high-level Large Language Model (LLM) research into how GPT-4 functioned at that time. It was also an on-going learning process throughout the project and as a team we had consistent dialogue while developers were testing prompts. This helped me get an understanding of any issues we encountered and how it may impact the user experience.

How can AI help pastors create sermons?

While AI is a valuable tool for pastors, it should complement, not replace, the essential elements of sermon creation — human touch, spiritual discernment and personal connection with the congregation.

Pastors benefit most when using AI as a supportive tool alongside their own insights and understanding.

UX Design

Information architecture and user flows

The challenge here was creating a logical, user-friendly structure for new concepts and mental models while aligning with the back-end data structure to ensure easy information retrieval. I used my user and competitor research along with stakeholder requirements to inform my design choices which we tested with paper wireframes.

Wireframes and Walkthroughs

We iterated through multiple rounds of paper sketches and walkthroughs during ideation. They provided a lightweight, flexible and cost-effective way to explore and communicate my design ideas. I also used them to present ideas for user flows and onboarding.

From paper to interactive prototypes

I developed multiple iterations of low-fi paper and then med-fi interactive XD prototypes. This allowed the whole team to visualize the functionality and onboarding process. We kept them med-fi so that I could easily incorporate stakeholder and developer input while testing feature functionality and made it easier to pivot when required.

Aligning language across headings, buttons, and navigation with industry-specific terminology was attempted, but there will be a need for a review and update once the system is completed and tested with real users. I intentionally kept some button labels generic due to the evolving functionality during the agile development.

UI design

Adding visual design and making it work as an interactive prototype

As an MVP we chose to spend reduced time on the look-and-feel and focus our attention on the functionality and user experience. With this in mind, I created a clean, neutral design ensuring that it looked appealing and aligned with the latest ui trends.

1. A single place to create and store your sermons

After registering for a free or paid plan, users will get access to a storage space and work tools to create sermon themes, outlines or full sermons. It ensures user-friendly creation, editing, seamless sharing, and effortless exportation of sermon content.

2. Enter a brief description with user specific criteria

By starting with a brief description of their theme and choosing a theological stance, the pastor is intentionally shaping the narrative of the sermon.

3. Theme overview is effortlessly generated

The AI-driven approach significantly reduces the time and effort traditionally spent on brainstorming and refining sermon themes. Pastors can now focus more on the nuanced aspects of sermon preparation, knowing that the AI has efficiently handled the foundational theme creation.

4. Fine tune, expand or edit any section of a sermon

This adaptability ensures that each sermon is finely tuned to convey the intended message, resonate with the congregation and address the diverse dynamics of worship and community life.

5. Modular sermon creation with customizable add-ons

PulpitPro simplifies sermon creation by breaking it into distinct parts: theme, outline, and full sermon. Pastors will later be able to upgrade to access add-ons like Kids Church sermons and song recommendations.

TAKEAWAY

Adapting to AI and navigating novelty

One of the primary challenges encountered in this project revolved around the novelty of AI technology in early 2023. Navigating uncharted territory presented unique obstacles requiring adaptability and creativity when conceptualising intuitive interfaces and user flows. Valuable lessons emerged, emphasizing the significance of collaboration within cross-functional teams while working with emerging technologies.

This project has changed my outlook as a designer, underscoring the importance of flexibility and adaptability due to the ever changing nature of GPT-4 and other LLMs. It also highlighted the necessity of a deep domain knowledge and the complexities surrounding carefully crafted prompts in order to deliver the best results using AI.

As of now, PulpitPro is in pre-shipment but pastors who have tested the demo are excited for it's launch.
